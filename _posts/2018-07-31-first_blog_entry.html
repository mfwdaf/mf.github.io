First Entry!

I have been working on getting Jekyll to work for hours, and I think I finally got it to work. If this content actually posts, I will know for sure. I have learned a few key takeaways from this exerience:

  • The people in charge of these projects assume a ton of development skill.
  • I do not have a ton of development skill :D
  • Git is the most confsing thing I have ever worked with and I'm still not sure I have it figured out.
  • Even creating a simple Jekyll page is not so much.

I have been on this coding journey for several months now, and some of the best resources are 100% free. However, I decided to purchase Colt Steele's Web Developer Bootcamp and Andrei Neagoie's The Complete Web Developer in 2018: Zero to Mastery from Udemy. This decision was based on a blog post from codeburst.io about becoming a developer in 2018, and I found the advice to be helpful. Both courses are good for their own reasons, but I like Colt's curriculum more, if only for the more personal feel of the examples, which include his dog, Rusty, and other relatable content. Before beginning either course, I had joined freeCodeCamp last year and I completed about 285 of the code challenges over the course of several months. I experienced some personal hardships over the past year that prevented me from completing the course as I had originally intended, but I do have plans to fulfill that promise to myself. At the time, I felt unprepared for the JavaScript examples, which is why I decided to diverge this year and take these two Udemy courses.

The point of this blog is to catalog my journey as suggested by Scott Hanselman when interviewed by Soran from the Code Newbie podcast. Plus, I really liked the idea he presented about reducing the amount of typing by creating repositories for your coding journey in the form of a blog and other media.

So for now, I will continue with my journey, and I will set a reminder to blog once a week as I continue to grow my web development knowledge.
